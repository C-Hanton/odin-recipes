Beef Bourguinon

Description

Beef bourguignon (a.k.a. beef Burgundy) is a French beef stew braised in red wine and beef broth. It also prominently features onions, carrots, and mushrooms. The dish has become synonymous with French cuisine and was famously a favorite of Julia Child.

Ingredients

  • Wine: For true beef bourguinon, you'll need a red burgundy wine that is produced in the burgundy region of eastern France with Pinot Noir grapes.
  • Brandy: Cognac brandy enhances the overall flavour of this complex dish
  • Fresh and canned vegetables: You'll need fresh onions, carrots and mushrooms. You'll also need a can of tomato paste.
  • Olive oil: You'll need olive oil for browning the beet and sauteing the mushrooms
  • Spice and seasonings: This traditional beef bourguinon is seasoned with parsely, a bay leaf, garlic, black peppercorns, garlic, salt and pepper.
  • Beef: This beef bourguinon recipe calls for two pounds of cubed beef chuck roast
  • Bacon: Bacon adds even more meaty flavour to this irresistible beef bourguinon
  • Flour: All-purpose flour helps thicken the bourguinon mixture
  • Broth: Use store-bought or homemade broth
  • Butter: Saute the mushrooms in butter before adding them to the meat mixture
